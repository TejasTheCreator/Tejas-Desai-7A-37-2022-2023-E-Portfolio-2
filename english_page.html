English Portfolio Activity 1: The Onge Tribe

The Onge are an Andamaneese tribe which live on the Andaman Islands. They were traditionally
hunter-gatherers and fishers, but recently, they also started cultivating crops. They live
on the Southern parts of the Andaman Islands. They speak the Onge Langauge, one of two known
southern Andamanese languages. It is spoken mostly on the southern part of the Andaman Islands.
The Onge follow the religion of Animism. Worshipping their ancestors, was a common and a important
element in the religious traditions of the Andaman Islands. In the 1900s, they had a total population
of about 672 however, as of 2011 it is estimated that they had a population of about 101, due to their
changes of food habits, during the 20th century. Hence, the Onge tribe is now classified as an endangered
tribe.
